Graduate Research Project

September 2022 – Present

Title: Multi-omics reveal the interactions among maize, fungal pathogen Bipolaris maydis, and microbiome

Supervisor: Dr. Kenichi Tsuda

Abstract: This research utilizes the maize-Bipolaris maydis-microbiome interaction system, aiming to reveal the molecular mechanisms of plant microbiome-mediated plant protection/pathogen promotion through metagenomics, transcriptomics, metabolomics as well as synthetic community.

Part of the National Key R&D Program of China (Project ID: SQ2022YFA1300114)

Undergraduate Research Project

September 2018 – June 2022

Title: Development of bioethanol detection type yeast sensor

Supervisor: Dr. Liangcai Peng

Abstract: Energy shortages and environmental pollution highlight the need for renewable energy. This project identified eight ethanol-specific inducible promoters (pESN1-8) in Pichia pastoris via RNA-seq, creating a "sensing yeast" that provides a basis for high-throughput ethanol detection and genetic improvement of ethanol-producing yeast.
